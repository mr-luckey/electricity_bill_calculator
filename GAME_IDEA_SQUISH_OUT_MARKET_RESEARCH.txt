1) EXECUTIVE VERDICT (READ THIS FIRST)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Recommended game to build:
  Name working titles: "Squish Out" / "Just Fit" / "Slip Out"
  Final recommended brand: SQUISH OUT

Why this idea (one sentence):
  Same addictive DNA as Arrows GO (one rule, one tap, order planning, satisfying
  exit animation, infinite difficulty with same rules) — but a BRAND NEW core
  rule the market has NOT turned into a chart-topping mobile category yet:
  pieces only leave if the path gaps are wide enough for their SIZE; removing
  small pieces opens necks so big pieces can finally slip out.

Market fit:
  - Arrow puzzles exploded in 2025–2026 (tens of millions of downloads / month)
  - Pure clones of arrows are already a bloody red ocean (Miniclip, Easybrain,
    Learnings / Amaze GO, etc.)
  - Best move is NOT another arrow clone — it is the NEXT simple order-puzzle
    with the same psychology but a fresh readable mechanic
  - US + Europe pay and retain best for calm hybrid-casual puzzles (women 25–44
    primary, men significant secondary, all ages adoptable)

Flutter fit: Excellent (2D grid logic + soft animations; Flame optional)

2) REFERENCE GAME BREAKDOWN — WHY ARROWS GO IS ADDICTIVE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Reference titles (2025–2026 wave):
  - Arrows GO! / later rebranded Amaze GO! (Learnings)
  - Arrows: Puzzle Escape (Lessmore / Miniclip ecosystem)
  - Arrow Puzzle (Easybrain / Miniclip)
  - Arrowscapes and many fast-follow clones

Core rule (learn in ~2 seconds from an ad):
  Tap an arrow. It flies off ONLY if nothing is blocking its path.
  Wrong order = stuck. Right order = clean board. Instant retry.

Why the brain gets hooked (psychology — reuse these in Squish Out):

  A) Zero-friction onboarding
     No tutorial walls. First level teaches by doing. Ad creatives ARE the tutorial.

  B) Instant competence
     Player feels smart within 10 seconds. Dopamine from "I get it."

  C) Order dependency (planning dopamine)
     Board is a dependency graph. Player simulates sequences in their head.
     "Aha!" when the right sequence unlocks.

  D) Sensory payoff (ASMR clear)
     Whoosh / slide / haptic. Disorder → empty clean board = mental reset.
     Especially strong for US/EU adult casual players seeking calm.

  E) Session length fit for mobile
     Typical level 30 seconds – 3 minutes. Perfect for commute, toilet, TV-couch,
     "one more before bed."

  F) Same rules, infinite depth
     No new verbs. Difficulty = denser dependencies. Retention stays high without
     teaching complex systems.

  G) Soft failure
     Fail is cheap. Retry is instant. No shame spiral → "just one more try."

  H) Ad-readable gameplay
     Silent vertical video of tapping and pieces leaving converts installs.
     Critical for Meta / Applovin / TikTok UA in US/EU.

What reviewers complain about (avoid these traps):
  - Unskippable interstitials every level destroy flow
  - No paid "Remove Ads" hurts sentiment (Arrows GO reviews often cite this)
  - Cluttered UI / dark noisy art kills the "calm clear" fantasy

Audience signal (Q1 2026 example — Arrows: Puzzle Escape US):
  - Skews female (~62%) but NOT only older women
  - Largest age band often 25–34, with strong 18–44 coverage
  - Broad international adoption, strong Western spenders

June 2026 chart reality (AppMagic / MobileGamer reporting):
  - Arrows: Puzzle Escape ~25.3M downloads in one month
  - Amaze GO ~23.2M
  - Arrow Puzzle ~16.4M
  - Arrow category temporarily beating long-running Block Blast momentum

Conclusion:
  The FORMULA is hot. The SPECIFIC mechanic (arrows) is saturated.
  Your job = keep the formula, change the mechanic.

3) MARKET LANDSCAPE 2025–2026 (DEEP RESEARCH SUMMARY)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 Macro mobile gaming
  - Mobile remains majority of global gaming revenue
  - Spend growth continues; downloads softer / flat in many regions
  - Western markets (North America + Europe) drive revenue quality
  - Europe cited as key growth driver in several 2025–2026 landscape reports
    (UK, Germany, France showing strong revenue gains)

3.2 Hyper-casual → Hybrid-casual shift (CRITICAL)
  Pure hyper-casual (ad-only, disposable, ultra-low LTV) is structurally weaker.
  Winning model in 2026 = HYBRID-CASUAL:

    Hyper-casual UX skin:
      - Instant play
      - One mechanic
      - Short levels
      - Ad-creative first design

    Casual depth layer:
      - Long level map
      - Soft meta (collection / garden / streak / daily)
      - Lives or soft energy (optional, carefully designed)
      - IAP + rewarded ads mix
      - Light LiveOps later (streaks, events, pass)

  Data signals:
  - Hyper-casual still huge on INSTALL share (~29% of gaming installs in 2025
    per Adjust-style reports) and sessions grew — demand for simple games is real
  - Hyper-casual REVENUE grew hard when titles hybridized (~80% class growth
    reported in AppMagic-style landscape notes for 2025) — LTV up, not just CPI
  - Hybrid/hyper D1 retention benchmarks near ~27% category averages; strong
    products aim higher (hybrid-casual targets often D1 35–45%, D7 ~18–22%,
    D30 ~8–12% as stretch goals for scalable paid UA)

3.3 Puzzle economy
  - Puzzle IAP market is multi-billion annually; Match-3 still dominates revenue
  - New entrants win by inventing NEW subgenres (Physical Match, Tile Match,
    Screw/Sort hybrids, then Arrow-order puzzles)
  - Deconstructor of Fun thesis: hybridcasual puzzles succeed when they keep
    hypercasual simplicity but add controlled unpredictability / depth without
    Match-3 booster complexity

3.4 Saturated mechanics (DO NOT CLONE AS PRIMARY PRODUCT)
  Already crowded / chart-proven — hard to win as a new solo studio without
  massive UA budget and better LiveOps:

  [SATURATED]
  - Arrow path-clear order puzzles (the current gold rush)
  - Block drag-match-clear (Block Blast and clones)
  - Screw / bolt / untangle
  - Water sort / color sort
  - Crowd / bus / seat color sorting
  - Hexa stack-sort
  - Classic Match-3
  - Parking / traffic unblock (mature)

  [EXISTS BUT NOT THE SAME CATEGORY HIT AS ARROWS]
  - Magnet place / swing physics puzzles (niche)
  - Rubber-band pin untangle (Flexy Ring etc.)
  - Browser ice-melt gravity order puzzles (not App Store category leaders)
  - Size-change parkour / obby (action, not calm board clear)

3.5 White space we are claiming
  NO major US/EU chart-dominating title was found that is:

    A calm, one-tap, board-clear ORDER puzzle
    where the blocking rule is SIZE vs GAP WIDTH
    (not direction arrows, not screws, not color sorting)

  Nearby concepts are either:
  - Platformers (shrink to fit corridor while running), OR
  - Resize tools (click walls to shrink/grow), OR
  - Physics ice-melt (browser niche)

  Squish Out occupies a clean blue pocket:
  Arrow-Go UX + Size-gated path logic + Soft "squish through" ASMR.

4) THE GAME IDEA — SQUISH OUT (COMPLETE DESIGN)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 One-line pitch (store / investor / ad)
  "Tap soft shapes so they slip off the board — but only if every gap on the path
   is wide enough. Clear small ones first to open the necks for the big ones."

4.2 Elevator pitch (30 seconds)
  Squish Out is a minimalist hybrid-casual puzzle. The board is full of cute soft
  blobs, each with a facing direction and a clear visual size (S / M / L / XL).
  Paths have narrow necks and wide halls. Tap a blob: if every neck along its
  exit path is wide enough, it squishes through with a satisfying stretch-and-pop
  animation and leaves. Empty cells open space and widen necks, unlocking bigger
  blobs. One rule. Infinite depth. Perfect for US/EU adults who love Arrows GO
  but want something NEW.

4.3 Core loop (30–90 second loop)
  LOOK → PLAN ORDER → TAP → SQUISH EXIT → BOARD CLEARS → REWARD → NEXT

  Micro rewards each tap:
  - Stretch animation through the neck
  - Soft pop when free
  - Haptic tick
  - Light particle trail
  - Board becomes visually calmer

4.4 Exact rules (player-facing — must fit on one screen)

  RULE 1: Every piece points a direction (tiny chevron or nose).
  RULE 2: Tap a piece to send it off the board in that direction.
  RULE 3: It can ONLY leave if every gap along that path is ≥ the piece’s size.
  RULE 4: When a piece leaves, its cell becomes empty; adjacent necks can widen
          (walls visually ease open into emptied space).
  RULE 5: Clear every piece to win. Undo / restart is free and instant.

  That is the entire game.

4.5 Why this is NEW (competitive differentiation)

  Arrows GO blocker type:     OBJECT presence (binary: blocked / clear)
  Squish Out blocker type:    GEOMETRY fit (size ≤ gap), even on "empty" cells

  Example that arrows cannot create:
  - Path cells empty, but a narrow door stops the XL blob
  - Two Small blobs must leave first to open the door for XL
  - Player learns "doors," not only "who's in front"

  Ad creative uniqueness:
  - Video shows a thick blob failing at a skinny neck (funny soft bump), then
    player taps tiny blobs, necks open, thick blob squishes out — instant laugh
    + "I want that"

4.6 Visual / audio / haptic fantasy (Western market)

  Art direction (avoid AI-generic purple/cream traps):
  - Soft clay / silicone toy look
  - Pastel but not washed-out: mint, coral, sky, butter — high clarity
  - Clean off-white playfield with gentle paper grain or soft radial wash
  - No dark cyber neon
  - No emoji spam
  - Minimal UI chrome

  Sound:
  - Rubber stretch
  - Soft wet "thoop" exit
  - Quiet whoosh
  - Optional calm lo-fi bed (toggleable; many US players mute — design for mute)

  Haptics:
  - Light impact on fail bump against too-narrow neck
  - Soft success pulse on clear

4.7 Level design pillars (addiction without new verbs)

  Tier 0 (levels 1–5): Teach one idea per level
    L1: One Small, wide path — free win
    L2: One Large fails on narrow neck; one Small opens it
    L3: Two directions + one shared neck
    L4: Order trap (tempting Large first fails)
    L5: First "aha" 5-piece maze

  Difficulty roller coaster (borrowed from winning casual puzzles):
    Easy → medium → hard spike → easy breath → medium → super hard → breath

  Introduction cadence for modifiers (ONE at a time, every 15–25 levels):
    - Turning corners (L-shaped exit path)
    - One-way necks (irreversible widen only after specific exits)
    - Twin necks (must clear both branches)
    - Locked size (piece that cannot leave until a "key small" unlocks a door
      tile — still same tap verb)
    - Ghost blockers (translucent props that shrink after N clears)
    - Color-tinted doors (only matching color widen — late game only)

  Hard rule for design:
    Never add a second primary verb. No drag-shoot, no place-tool, no paint.
    Boosters only as monetization later, never as required skill.

4.8 Fail / assist design (retention critical)

  - Unlimited free undo OR unlimited restart (prefer both)
  - Fail = piece bumps and squashes against neck (comedy), snaps back
  - After 3 fails on same level: optional free hint silhouette (watch ad OR spend)
  - Dynamic softening AFTER many fails (quietly widen one neck) — reduces churn
    without telling the player they were "babied"

4.9 Meta progression (hybrid layer — keep LIGHT)

  Phase 1 (MVP — 2–4 weeks core):
    - Linear map of 100–150 handcrafted levels
    - Stars for clean clears (optional: fewer undos = more stars)
    - Daily gift

  Phase 2 (retention):
    - Soft "workshop" shelf: collect squish toys / shelves as cosmetic meta
    - Win streak multiplier (calming, not aggressive FOMO)
    - Daily challenge board

  Phase 3 (LiveOps when metrics good):
    - Season of new neck types / themes
    - Soft pass (cosmetics + hints)
    - Events: "Open 20 necks today"

  Do NOT ship a heavy city-builder if it delays prototype. Meta after CPI test.

8) MONETIZATION (HYBRID MODEL FOR US/EU)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Industry reality 2026:
  - Western puzzle hybrids often land near ~45/55 to 50/50 IAP vs ads (varies)
  - Rewarded video is sacred (player choice)
  - Interstitials must protect flow or reviews die
  - Remove Ads IAP is table stakes (Arrows GO pain point)

Recommended MVP economy:

  Ads:
  - Rewarded: hint, reveal one valid next piece, +undo pack, x2 coins
  - Interstitial: every 2–3 WINS (A/B), NEVER on fail restart
  - Banner: optional bottom on hub only, NOT during active puzzle (focus kill)

  IAP (USD example ladder for US/UK):
  - Remove Ads — $4.99 (or $3.99 intro)
  - Starter Pack — $0.99–$1.99 (hints + remove interstitial 3 days)
  - Hint Pack S/M/L — $1.99 / $4.99 / $9.99
  - Cosmetic themes (clay packs) — $1.99–$2.99
  - Optional soft weekly pass later — $4.99

  Ethics / long-term ratings:
  - Never block progress behind forced IAP for early levels
  - Always offer rewarded alternative for hints
  - Publish clear Remove Ads that actually removes interstitials AND banners

LTV targets to justify US UA (directional):
  - Early organic/soft launch: learn retention before heavy spend
  - US CPI for puzzles often multi-dollar; without hybrid LTV you die
  - Aim toward D30 LTV that can support Tier-1 ROAS; if not, fix retention first

10) FLUTTER TECHNICAL PLAN (WHY THIS IS BUILDABLE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

10.1 Why Flutter is enough
  - Pure 2D puzzle, no 3D world needed
  - Hot reload = fastest level iteration
  - Small APK vs Unity for this genre
  - Official Flutter casual games pathway + Flame engine if needed
  - Single codebase iOS/Android (critical for indie/solo + small team)

10.2 Suggested stack
  - Flutter 3.x + Dart
  - Flame (optional but recommended for game loop, input, particles)
  - Flutter overlays for Hub / Shop / Settings (Flame Overlay API)
  - CustomPainter or Flame components for soft blob mesh / stretch shaders
  - audioplayers or flame_audio
  - shared_preferences / hive for progress
  - Google Mobile Ads / Applovin MAX mediation
  - in_app_purchase
  - Firebase Analytics + Crashlytics + Remote Config (difficulty knobs)
  - Cloud drafts later for LiveOps config

10.3 Core data model (simple)
  Level JSON:
    grid size
    walls / neck widths per edge
    pieces[] { id, x, y, size, dir, color, skin }

  Solver (offline editor tool):
    BFS / recursive search to verify every shipped level is solvable
    Also generate "hint first move" for rewarded hints
    CRITICAL: never ship unsolvable levels

10.4 Animation (the product IS the feel)
  - Squish through neck: scale X/Y squash + stretch along path
  - Fail bump: elastic overshoot back
  - Neck widen: subtle wall morph when cell frees
  Target 60 FPS on mid Android

10.5 MVP scope (8–12 weeks solo / small team estimate)
  Week 1–2: Core rule prototype + 20 levels + juice
  Week 3–4: 80–120 levels + undo/restart + hubs
  Week 5–6: Ads + IAP Remove Ads + analytics
  Week 7–8: Soft launch polish, creative capture, bug bash
  Then: US/UK soft launch → iterate retention → scale

12) RISKS & MITIGATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Risk: Players say "it's just Arrows with fat blobs"
  Mitigation: Emphasize FAIL-ON-EMPTY-PATH-BUT-NARROW-NECK in first 30s of every
  creative and in levels 2–4. Make necks visually obvious.

Risk: Arrow megastudios clone you in 30 days
  Mitigation: Ship fast, own ASO terms, build level volume + meta + brand juice,
  keep updating weekly. First-mover of a NEW verb still wins attention window.

Risk: Difficulty cliff churn
  Mitigation: Roller-coaster cadence + dynamic assist after fails + free undo.

Risk: Ad aggression review bomb
  Mitigation: Interstitial spacing + Remove Ads day-one + rewarded-first culture.

Risk: Flutter performance on low devices
  Mitigation: Object pool particles, limit overdraw, test on budget Androids.

13) ALTERNATIVE BACKUP IDEAS (IF PROTOTYPE TEST FAILS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Only use if Squish Out playtests fail the "instant understand" test.

ALT A — PAIR EXIT
  Linked twins must both have clear exits; tapping one attempts both.
  Unique, but slightly harder to read in ads.

ALT B — PRESSURE RELEASE
  Pieces leave only at safe pressure; neighbors change pressure when one leaves.
  Risk: harder tutorial.

ALT C — BEAM DOCK
  Piece leaves only if its light beam reaches border; still too close to arrows.

Recommendation: Prototype Squish Out first. Do not split focus.
